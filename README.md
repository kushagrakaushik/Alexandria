# Alexandria

Book tracking and discovery platform prototype.

## Problem Statement

Readers use multiple apps for discovery, tracking, and reviews. Existing tools often feel cluttered, generic, and weak on personalized motivation.

## How Might We

- How might we make tracking progress feel effortless and visually rewarding?
- How might we personalize discovery without over-collecting user data?
- How might we help readers quickly decide what to read next?
- How might we create lightweight social motivation without pressure?
- How might we make the UI feel calm, modern, and book-focused?

## Empathy Map (Expanded)

### Says

- "I have too many recommendations and I do not know where to start."
- "I keep forgetting which books I paused halfway."
- "I want quick suggestions, not 20 minutes of scrolling."

### Thinks

- "If I set an unrealistic reading goal, I will quit again."
- "My taste is changing and generic bestseller lists do not fit me anymore."
- "I do not want friends to see every book I drop."

### Does

- Screenshots recommendations from Instagram and saves them in notes.
- Starts 3 to 4 books in parallel and abandons at least one.
- Checks reviews after reading 30 to 40 pages before deciding to continue.

### Feels

- Overwhelmed by choice, especially after long breaks from reading.
- Guilty when progress streaks break.
- Quietly proud when finishing a book but not always comfortable sharing publicly.

## Public vs Private Reading Behavior

- Publicly says: "I read whatever interests me."
- Privately thinks: "I avoid certain genres because I worry people will judge my taste."

## User Testing Snapshot

5-minute hallway test with 3 readers:

- All users understood shelf tracking in under 20 seconds.
- 2 users expected genre buttons to also filter already-saved library items.
- 1 user asked for a clearer distinction between "Want to Read" and "Reading" actions.

## Prototype Notes

- App is pre-populated with realistic sample books for demo evaluation.
- Featured book and shelves now align with the fantasy/sci-fi/mystery/romance persona.
- Library stats and recent activity are non-empty on first load.
